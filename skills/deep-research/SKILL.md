# Deep Research

Autonomous multi-step research producing detailed, cited reports.

## When to Use

- Market analysis and competitive landscaping
- Technical literature reviews
- Due diligence research
- Comparing tools, frameworks, or approaches
- Any research requiring multiple sources and synthesis

## Workflow

### 1. Understand the Question
Break down the research question into sub-questions that need answering.

### 2. Multi-Source Search
For each sub-question:
- Use WebSearch for broad discovery
- Use WebFetch for deep reading of specific sources
- Cross-reference findings across sources
- Note contradictions between sources

### 3. Synthesize
- Organize findings by theme, not by source
- Highlight consensus views vs minority opinions
- Include specific numbers, dates, names
- Flag areas of uncertainty

### 4. Report
Structure the output as:
- **Executive Summary** (2-3 sentences)
- **Key Findings** (numbered, factual)
- **Detailed Analysis** (organized by theme)
- **Limitations** (what couldn't be verified)

## Guidelines

- Prioritize recent sources (last 12 months)
- Cross-reference claims across multiple sources
- Include specific data points over vague claims
- Clearly separate facts from opinions
- If conflicting info, present both sides
- Never fabricate citations or data points
